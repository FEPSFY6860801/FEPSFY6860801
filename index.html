Introduction

      

	      In this project, I aim to investigate the extent to which supersonic transport is the
      future of aviation, and to assess the sustainability of the business model of supersonic
      transport.
	      Supersonic transport first appeared in the 1960s, when demand for air travel
      skyrocketed and thus the need for a new type of high-capacity aircraft was needed.
      Europe responded with Concorde, and America responded with the Boeing 747. The
      Concorde was an excellent proof of concept, but was never as successful as the 747, and
      as a result only 20 were built. The last flight of Concorde was in 2003 and took 11 years
      for a replacement to be dreamed up.
	      The Boom Overture seems to be the likely successor to Concorde, with it already
      receiving firm orders from both United and American Airlines, with options on aircraft
      from Japan Airlines, the Virgin Group and others.
